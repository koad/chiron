# Level 9: Bond Governance in Practice — Renewal, Scope Negotiation, Living with Imperfect Bonds

## Learning Objective

After completing this level, the operator will be able to:
> Manage the operational lifecycle of a trust bond — schedule annual renewal, negotiate scope changes without revoking the active bond, recognize when an entity is operating in a gap between its bond's written scope and a real situation, and know which gaps require a new bond vs. which can be resolved by the entity using judgment within its mandate.

**Why this matters:** Trust bonds are living documents in an operating ecosystem. The scope written on day one will not perfectly match situations on day ninety. Operators who treat bonds as static — once signed, never revisited — end up with either stale bonds that don't reflect current operations or an escalation backlog where the entity cannot act without human intervention. The art of bond governance is keeping bonds current enough to be functional while not re-issuing bonds constantly.

## Knowledge Atoms

### Atom 9.1: Annual Renewal — What It Is and What It Isn't

**Teaches:** What the annual renewal in the bond's `renewal` field actually requires: a review of scope, a re-signing if scope has changed, a renewal commitment if scope is unchanged. Renewal is not a bureaucratic formality — it is a forcing function for scope review. If the entity's operations have expanded and the bond hasn't been updated, renewal is when that mismatch is corrected.

### Atom 9.2: Scope Gaps — When the Entity Needs to Act but the Bond Is Silent

**Teaches:** The practical situation where an entity encounters a task that the bond does not explicitly authorize or prohibit. The decision framework: (1) Is the action consistent with the bond's stated purpose? (2) Does it fit within the spirit of the authorized actions? (3) Would the issuer approve if asked? If all three — the entity may proceed and document the decision. If any are uncertain — escalate before acting.

### Atom 9.3: The Bond as a Living Document — Version History, Comments, and Notes

**Teaches:** How to maintain a bond as it evolves: appending amendment notes (not replacing the original text), versioning the bond document, the convention for noting amendments in the signing block. A bond that has been amended is still the same bond — the original signature covers the original text; amendments are acknowledged separately.
